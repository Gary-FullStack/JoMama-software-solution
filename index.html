Not a freedom without consequence

Freedom of speech is not absolute. There are some limitations on speech that are necessary to protect other important values, such as national security, public safety, and the rights of others. For example, the government can restrict speech that is considered to be incitement to violence, obscenity, or defamation. However, these limitations are narrowly drawn and must be justified by a compelling government interest.

In general, the government should err on the side of allowing speech, even if it is offensive or unpopular.
